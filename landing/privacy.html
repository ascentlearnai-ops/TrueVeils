Privacy notice

Interview evidence should be clear before the session starts.

Truveil is a consent-based interview verification product for technical hiring teams. This notice explains the information Truveil is designed to process during a verified interview.

Information processed during a session

  • Session details such as recruiter, candidate name, role, join code, policy preset, allowed destinations, and restricted destinations.
  • Live transcript text, transcript source, timestamps, confidence metadata when available, and response-window analysis context.
  • Foreground application names, window titles, browser host or URL when Windows exposes it, detection source, matched policy rule, and restricted-target events.
  • Interviewer notes, bookmarks, follow-up prompts, report metadata, and review-band output.

Audio handling

Truveil is designed to use microphone audio for live transcription. Raw audio is not included in reports. Temporary fallback audio, when used, should be deleted after transcription or failure handling.

Information not intentionally collected

  • No camera video.
  • No screen recording.
  • No file-system access to candidate documents.
  • No clipboard collection.
  • No browser-history scraping outside the active foreground detection available during the session.

Retention

Reports and transcript evidence are retained for up to 30 days by default during beta unless the customer deletes them sooner or requests deletion through support.

Customer responsibilities

Customers are responsible for giving candidates appropriate notice, obtaining consent, configuring policies fairly, and using Truveil evidence as human-reviewed context rather than an automated hiring decision.

Requests

For deletion, privacy, or support requests, contact support@truveil.com.

This beta notice is provided for product transparency and does not replace legal advice for your organization.
