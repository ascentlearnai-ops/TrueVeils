Beta terms

Terms for evidence-first interview verification.

These beta terms describe acceptable use of Truveil Command Center and Truveil Secure during pilot and paid-beta use.

Advisory evidence only

Truveil produces interview evidence for human review. It does not determine whether a candidate violated policy, whether a candidate is truthful, or whether a candidate should be hired. Customers must not use Truveil as the sole basis for an employment decision.

Required candidate notice and consent

Before starting a session, customers must provide candidates with appropriate notice and obtain consent for microphone transcription, foreground app/window monitoring, restricted-target controls, report retention, and any other use required by applicable law or customer policy.

Best-effort desktop controls

Restricted-target closure, tab detection, app detection, and browser URL detection are best-effort Windows desktop controls. Truveil does not guarantee browser-level blocking, complete detection, or prevention of all interview misconduct.

Fair review

Customers are responsible for reviewing all evidence fairly, considering transcript quality and telemetry health, and giving reviewers appropriate training before relying on reports.

Data handling

Customers must not upload unnecessary sensitive personal information to Truveil. Reports are retained for up to 30 days by default during beta unless deleted sooner.

No regulated-use guarantee

Truveil is not a legal, compliance, employment-law, or background-check service. Customers are responsible for determining whether their use of Truveil is permitted in their jurisdiction and hiring workflow.

These beta terms are a product template and should be reviewed by qualified counsel before production customer contracts.
